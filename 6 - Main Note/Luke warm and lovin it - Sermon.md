Laodicea, a wealthy city in ancient Phrygia,

**Ellen G. White’s Final Plea**:

> _“The Laodicean message… is a testing truth. It will separate the chaff from the wheat.”_ (_Early Writings_, p. 270).

Maybe as you’ve listened today, you’ve realized something. Maybe you’ve noticed that your walk with Jesus has grown casual, routine—even lukewarm. You still believe, you still come to church, but the fire that once burned in your heart feels dim.

Friend, Jesus sees that. He’s not here to shame you—He’s here to call you back. He says in Revelation 3, _“I stand at the door and knock.”_ That means He’s right at the door of **your** heart, waiting. He’s not far away, not unreachable—He’s right here, asking, “Will you let Me in fully? Will you let Me light the fire again?”

Being lukewarm is dangerous, because it feels comfortable. But comfort can numb us until it’s too late. Jesus is pleading with us not to settle for half a faith, half a love, half a surrender. He wants you—all of you.

Today, you can make a choice. You can say, “Lord, I don’t want to stay lukewarm. I want to burn with Your Spirit. I want my life to count for eternity.” Maybe that means surrendering a hidden habit. Maybe it means making time for prayer again. Maybe it means stepping out in faith to serve or share your testimony.

Whatever it is for you, Jesus is calling. This is your moment. Don’t put Him off. Don’t wait for a more convenient time. Open the door now.
