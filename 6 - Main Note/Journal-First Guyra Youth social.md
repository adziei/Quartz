# Journal-First Guyra Youth social

On the 6th of September 2025 I hosted the first Adventist Guyra Youth social meeting, an event solely dedicated to socialise with many different kinds of youth and to provide a safe space where no one will be judged for who they are. I hosted this event at the Roller skating rink and bowling alley, the preperation of this event was quite stressful as it was the first event for my social action project and it went something like this,

Firstly I needed to make a google calendar with all the events that I presented at a church board meeting and business meeting, after this I had to call or ask in person whether they would like to get notified by themselves or by a parent, this is were I encountered my first issue when organising these type of events, some parents would get notified of an event happening but then would proceed to not tell the youth until the night before, and If I emailed the youth or talked with them in person a lot of them would say they will come and then find out later that their parents refused to let them go or just simply not notify their parents of the event at all, simple fix right? just tell both of them? yeah that is what I believed at first until I had a discussion where they chose that an in person invitation will work better than a digital.., I then encounterd another problem the fact they I told them in person and then they would refuse to acknowledge the rememberance of said action. it kept on going in circles until I figured I could make a social media account on facebook and on instagram to promote the events and post an update that everyone with a cellular device could see if they just follow the account and enable notifications for it. and now it works everyone is happy for this to go through as the way of communication but needs if any particular person needs more information they can either ask in person, email, or just phone call. 

The last thing I did to advertise this event was to announce it at the church during the annoucement period to remind anyone in case they forgot or to remind any others who are not aware of the event happening by some chance.
